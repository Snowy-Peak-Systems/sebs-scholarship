Who We Are

Founded by the SEBS class of 2017, the SEBS Scholarship Foundation is a non-profit public charity dedicated to helping SEBS students afford to further their education. We do this by providing yearly scholarships to graduating SEBS seniors who demonstrate characteristics that align with our core values.

We aim to benefit students who demonstrate the following characteristics:

  • Persist in the face of challenges, both academic and personal.
  • Desire to improve the world around them.
  • Maintain an uncompromising character that demonstrates strength, respect, and honesty.
  • Demonstrate leadership potential and emotional maturity.
  • Shows enthusiasm in learning and takes initiative in one's own education.

We believe that the education earned at SEBS prepares students for a lifetime of success, should they choose to apply all theyâ€™ve learned. However, soon after graduation we learned just how expensive higher education is, yet we recognize its importance for those wanting to take the next step towards their career. Because of this, we decided to start the SEBS Scholarship, with the intention of lessening the financial burden on the students and families of those graduating from SEBS.

To the graduating seniors at SEBS —
If you believe you possess the above characteristics, we strongly encourage you to apply to our scholarship. We look forward to learning more about you and wish you all the best in your college endeavors.

Eligibility Requirements


The minimum requirements needed to apply for this scholarship are as follows:

  • Be a currently enrolled senior graduating from the School of Engineering and Biomedical Science at Pueblo County High School.

  • Hold a cumulative weighted GPA of at least 3.0 at the time of application.

  • Student must enroll in college in the fall semester following their graduation year.

Application Requirements


The application includes two creative responses and your high school transcript. Listed below are all the required parts of the application.

  • Upload your latest unofficial high school transcript as a single PDF document.

  • In 500 words or fewer, tell us about a specific topic or issue related to your intended career that you are passionate about. What about this topic makes it so interesting to you? How is this topic/issue significant to the future of your intended field? How is this relevant to the average person?

  • In 500 words or fewer, describe a moment in your life where you made a mistake or felt disappointed in yourself. What did you learn about yourself through this experience? How will you use this experience to improve your life and the lives of others in your career, community, or personal life?
